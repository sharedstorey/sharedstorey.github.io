Dianna & Aiden

Our Story

Several years ago, Aiden packed his bags and moved from New Zealand to Canada on a whim. One night while having dinner at a Toronto diner, he made friends with some of the servers there – including Dianna’s best friend, Kaelee. Kaelee, a Toronto native, showed Aiden around the city over the next few days and even ended up finding him a place to live.


When she later told Dianna about making friends with one of her customers – a rare occurrence – Kaelee said, “If you weren’t in a relationship with someone right now, I’d tell you I met the perfect guy for you.”


Kaelee pestered both Aiden and Dianna over the next year because she really wanted the two to meet, despite the fact that they were both in relationships with other people. When they finally did meet, Dianna wondered if her friend’s instincts had been right all along...

FAQ

Q: Do you have a gift registry?

Your presence at our wedding is present enough! However, for friends and family who have expressed an interest, we’ve created an online registry with The Bay and Crate and Barrel.

Q: Can I bring a date/family member?

Due to COVID restrictions and venue capacity, our wedding is strictly RSVP-only. We can only accommodate those listed on your invitation.

Q: Are kids welcome?

While we love your little ones, our wedding is an adults-only event so that everyone can relax and enjoy the evening. We appreciate you making arrangements ahead of time and leaving the kids at home so you can celebrate with us.

Q: Where should I park?

There are several parking garages on both King Street West and Spadina Avenue.

Q: What is the dress code?

The event is black tie optional, so feel free to dust off your spiffiest suit!

Q: Where can I stay if I'd like a hotel near the venue?

Information coming soon.

Q: What's your wedding hashtag?

Please use #fahelofastorey when posting on social media (special thanks to our friend Aaron for coming up with it!).

Q: What are the COVID restrictions?

Please note, the Government of Ontario requires that all attendees of the wedding be fully vaccinated. Venue staff will be checking for your proof of vaccination or medical exemption at the door upon arrival. "Fully vaccinated" in Ontario means having had two doses of an approved vaccine (Pfizer, Moderna, AstraZeneca, or J&J), with the most recent at least 14 days prior to the wedding. Neither a recent COVID-19 infection nor a recent negative test will substitute for proof of two shots plus 14 days.

If you are not vaccinated or cannot obtain a medical exemption, please RSVP that you will not be coming (we will miss you!) or reach out to us directly before you RSVP.
